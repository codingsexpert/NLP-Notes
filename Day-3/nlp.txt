
•	Lowercasing: Converting all text to lowercase to ensure consistency, as machines treat capitalized and lowercase words as distinct entities, which adds unnecessary complexity.
•	Removing Noise: Cleaning raw data by stripping out extraneous elements such as HTML tags and URLs, which are typically irrelevant for tasks like sentiment analysis.

•	Chat Word Treatment: Converting informal slang and abbreviations (e.g., 'GN' to 'good night') into standard language.
•	Spelling Correction: Utilizing libraries like TextBlob to identify and fix common typographical errors.
•	Stop Words Removal: Filtering out high-frequency, low-information words like "the," "is," or "in" to allow the model to focus on meaningful content.
•	Handling Emojis: Strategies to either remove emojis entirely or map them to their descriptive text meanings using emoji-handling modules.
•	Tokenization: The process of breaking down text into smaller, meaningful units (tokens) such as words or sentences, which is essential for feature engineering.
•	Stemming vs. Lemmatization: Techniques for reducing words to their root forms:
•	Stemming: A faster, rule-based approach that chops off suffixes; however, it may result in non-dictionary words.
•	Lemmatization: A more accurate, dictionary-based approach (using WordNet) that returns valid linguistic root words, though it is computationally slower.
